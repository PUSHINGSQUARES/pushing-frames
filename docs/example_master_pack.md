# Notes

Lead with "Shot on [camera]" or "Shot on [camera] mounted on [rig]" at the start of the action description. State T-stop and shutter speed. Include fps only when motion blur is part of the read (e.g. 1/48s at 24fps for the classic cinematic shutter angle). Naming the specific prime length (24mm, 50mm, 85mm) biases the field of view and depth of field more reliably than a generic description.

For rigs, be specific: Steadicam, U-Crane (Russian Arm), Black Arm tracking buggy, helicopter gimbal, FPV drone. The model reads rig terminology as camera movement cues more reliably than plain "tracking shot".

Anamorphic lenses unlock elliptical bokeh, streak flares, and complex chromatic aberration in dust and spray. State the squeeze ratio (2x). For spherical lenses on ARRI, add "Zeiss Master Primes" or "Leica Summicron-C" when you want clinical sharpness over character glass.

Lighting state calls: "morning golden hour", "harsh midday", "late afternoon raking light", "overcast diffused". Heat haze reads best when called out explicitly in medium and wide shots. For interiors, name the light quality ("mixed practicals and daylight spill through window left").

Film stock calls in the STYLE_GUIDE block carry over to every shot by default. Override per-shot only when you want a deliberate break—e.g. a POV helmet cam that should feel like GoPro rather than Alexa.
