# Shots

<!--
SHOT 1 — paddock_awakening (Act 1, atmosphere)
WHY ARRI Alexa Mini LF + Steadicam: the LF sensor reads "premium
feature film" to the model better than ARRI Mini or RED. Steadicam
gives the smooth push-in that establishes the world without feeling
documentary.
WHY 24mm at f/2.8: wide enough to hold the cars and the figure walking
in, shallow enough to bias focus to the hero (golden-hour rim catches
the M-stripe).
WHY golden hour: the entire act is "anticipation morning"—calling the
hour explicitly stops the model defaulting to noon-flat establishing.
-->

<!--
SHOT NOTES (per shot, the rationale that didn't fit in the table):

paddock_awakening — Steadicam push-in is the right opener for the
"world reveals itself" feel. Wide aspect 21:9 because cinematic. The
protagonist walking toward camera-frame works better than walking away.

tactile_prep — Macro shows the build quality of the Alcantara—an
M-car detail that justifies the price point. The hands_on_wheel.jpg
ref is critical, this is the shot that breaks without a real ref.

epic_track_wide — The 14mm ultra-wide on Alexa 65 is the "feature
film opening titles" lens combination. Deep focus at f/8 keeps the
landscape and the car both readable. Long afternoon shadows are the
visual rhyme to golden hour in shot 1.

wet_skid_drift — Telephoto 135mm compresses the spray, isolates the
car. Slow-motion at 120fps is the only way water physics reads at
all—at 24fps it just looks like a smear.

visor_pov — The hardest shot in the original brief. Seedream 5.4
failed first attempts by rendering the helmet from outside. The fix
is the explicit "INSIDE the helmet, padding frames the edge"
phrasing. Worth keeping verbatim.

cool_down — 85mm at T1.2 is the portrait look that sells "we earned
this". Heat haze rippling off the hood is the visual callback to
shot 3. Cycles back to the protagonist for closure.
-->
